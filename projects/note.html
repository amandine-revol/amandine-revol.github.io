Allowing teams to collaborate in Levuro to increase App Engagement & User Retention

Introduction

About the company

Levuro is a social media management tool that allows users to manage their social media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Linkedin, Youtube) in one place.

Problem statement

Through user feedback, we noticed that teams using Levuro could not use the tool efficiently to collaborate: asking for review or feedback on the post they wrote. Instead, they used long chains of emails or online text editors with comments, making the process long and tedious.

Solution

We designed a new feature to allow users to exchange instant messages to communicate about their work. Users can easily ask their teammates to review their social media posts and get feedback.

Roles and responsibilities

UX designers team of one. I manage the entire process including :

  • Wireframes

  • User testing for iterations
  • Presentations to the team to gather feedback and validation

Research: user interviews

User interviews revealed a struggle to work as a team in Levuro.

During this phase, I interviewed eight users to understand their way of working and needs. I also interviewed users working alone to see if they could also have a need for this feature.

Insight #1
Users struggle to have their work reviewed.

“Sometimes my manager will review my posts. We do that by email, and I hate it. It’s a tedious process. Sometimes I can't see the changes because he forgot to highlight it. I lose track. If there is a way to do that in Levuro that would be great.”

"Usually, I publish without asking Jinan. Sometimes we will discuss the post by email. It can quickly get very complicated if we are more than two."

"I will usually send a screenshot from the Levuro preview to my clients, and we'll discuss it by email. We can have a lot of back and forth before agreeing to a final post."

Insight #2
Users preferred the concept of mentioning specific members of their team instead of assigning. They would like an organized and centralized space to exchange about the work.
Insight #3
Users would like to be able to attach a note or a file in addition to their comments.
Insight #4
Users saw a value. Even if working alone, they could write something to themself and use it as a to-do list or memo

Research: competitive analysis

Competitive Analysis showed that the collaboration in other tools is either a rigid approval concept or non-existent.

I analyzed four direct competitors that allow teams to collaborate on their tools. It helped me gain insights into how each company designed their feature and possibly identify opportunities for how to create a better solution.

Learnings:

  • Two competitors have a strict approval user flow, possibly having several approver levels. Approvers can directly approve/reject or edit the post themselves.
  • The concept of Notes is interesting. It keeps it light but doesn't allow collaboration since it's only a single note on the media.
  • None of the platforms have a system where a team can write comments and discuss their posts (similar to google docs).

Research: use cases

Identification of use cases to help design a feature that could work for most users.

I gathered the team (CEO, three developers, and our social media manager) to brainstorm the different ways users could use the collaboration feature tool. Some use cases came directly from our users. Clear use cases help frame my work by understanding how users will need to use the features and their goals

Use case #1
My permissions are restricted: I can only draft posts. I need to be able to tell someone this post is ready to be published.
Use case #2
I want to tell the team photographer to add a media to this post.
Use case #3
I draft a post and want to add notes for myself as a memo.
Use case #4
I want to ask someone to proofread my post.
Use case #5
I see that a scheduled post has a typo in the feed view. I want to communicate with the person that made the post
Use case #6
I am an agency, and I want to offer my clients an easy way to give me their feedback.

Design: prototyping different solutions

I prototyped different concepts to be reviewed with the team and choose which one could work best for our users.

I made two prototypes to help the team to get an idea of how the collaboration in Levuro could work. And help choose the direction to go before validating with user testing. The wireframes helped answer:

  • Should we use an approval flow where the reviewers can approve or reject?
  • Should we offer the possibility for users to write notes/comments next to the post they are writing, with the option for teams to communicate with a chat (similar to google docs)?
  • Where in the web app should users be able to communicate/collaborate?
  • How can the existing interface could integrate the concept of communication and comments?

Prototypes were made using elements of the design system. That’s why they look like high-fidelity prototypes.

Option 1: Approval concept with a button in the composer to assign someone as a reviewers.

Option 2: Concept of note and chat in a sidebar.

We decided to explore the concept of notes in the form of live chat between team members without a strict approval workflow, giving flexibility to users.

  • Assigned was judged too rigid and complex by the team for a first integration in the tool. We decided that the concept of Notes could cover users' needs when working in a group or alone. We also decided to test a version with the idea of a chat.
  • Users should be able to collaborate when they write a post (= in the composer). They should also be able to leave a note where draft and scheduled posts are displayed (in the feed and calendar view).

With the team aligned on the main idea, I could refine my work and made three clickable prototypes for user testing.

User testing

User Testing revealed a considerable preference for option 2 but also some vulnerabilities. The next step was to present my findings to the team, the problems found and proposed solutions.

Learning #1
All users preferred prototype 2. They liked the option to be able to expand the area where they type their text.
Learning #2
Users need to be able to type their notes and still have a view of the post they are writing. Option 3 with the modal did not work for most users.
Learning #3
Users want what they are used to. Option 3 with the dropdown to mention someone had an adverse reaction. Users just wanted to use the “@” to mention someone. And could not see the utility of a dropdown menu.
Learning #4
The notes area was not visible enough for most users. If the educational content was expanded the space for the notes was limited.
ULearning #5
Users liked the concept of chat to communicate efficiently. And the toolbar where they could add an emoji, and mention someone. They see it important to be able to attach a file or a media to their comment.
Problems Solutions
Notes are not visible enough.
Because of all the features in the composer, user had difficulties seeing the notes area. 		1) Display of a placeholder note with an emoji to make it more visible. The text could give users tips.
2) Display all tools on the left side using a vertical toolbar (see illustration below).
"Send now" is scary for users. Some think it would send the comment to their social media platforms. Replace the text of the tooltip with "Add notes".
Problems Solutions
Users want to add media or files when writing a note.
 Add the option to attach a file in the toolbar. Users should also be able to drag and drop their files or media.
When editing their comments, users still want to use the toolbar to add emojis, mention or attach a file.
Add these options in the editing view: emoji and attach a file.

Final design

With the learnings from the users test, we could define with the team a final prototype and go deep into details in the functionalities of this feature.

The vertical toolbar was adopted, allowing the composer to stay clean and organized. We also defined how Notes should look in the feed and calendar views. We extended the concept to the composer to remain consistent. We also introduced notifications to alert users of any new notes/comments.

Conclusion

A positive impact on our existing users, new users and the company.

Since the free version of Levuro includes an unlimited number of users per workspace, we needed a strong collaboration feature to allow teams to do their best work directly inside Levuro. Eliminating the need for an additional tool.

  • Since the implementation of this collaborative feature, new accounts with multiple users increased by 15%.
  • User feedback revealed that most teams are now giving each other feedback in Levuro instead of using emails.
  • Existing accounts with multiple users showed more time spent on the tool and an active use of this new feature.
