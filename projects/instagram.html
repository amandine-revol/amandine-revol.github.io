Redesigning Levuro's main failure path: The connection to an Instagram profile and the definition of a publishing method.

Introduction

About the company

Levuro is a social media management platform and live stream management tool for Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and YouTube. Users can publish through the web app or the mobile app.

Problem statement

More than half of Levuro users have an Instagram account, but we realized that many struggle to connect their Instagram profiles to Levuro and can’t define how to publish their posts. As a result, many users are abandoning the platform or deleting their accounts. Improving the user experience will help reduce customer churn, which is very high.

Solution

We changed the user flow to allow users to decide how they wanted to publish on social media. We re-wrote most of the text to provide users with more guidance and removed jargon. We also removed barriers to allow users to use Levuro and draft their posts before defining a publishing method.

Roles and responsibilities

As a solo UX designer, I managed the project by identifying the user's pain points and bringing them to the team's attention and taking responsibility to fix it. I led additional research with usability testing, designed the new user flow and design improvements, organized cross-functional meetings to brainstorm and sync with the team, and made high-fidelity prototypes for hand-off to the development team.

This project was collaborative and agile, with a cross-functional team (2 developers, 1 CEO/ sales, and me). I leveraged everyone's expertise and knowledge to make the best decisions.

Context

How we discovered the problem?

Once a month, the team meets to discuss how we could improve the product. Several insights led me to think that something was wrong with our Instagram users and that we should investigate further:

  • Beta users left the program because of frustration with publishing on Instagram.
  • There is a high demand from users for support in defining a publishing method for Instagram.
  • Data analytics revealed that Instagram users represented a big part of deleted accounts.


To stop losing more users, we grouped all the information we had to identify pain points patterns and reduce users' frustrations.

Impact on users

The graphs show that half of Levuro’s users have an Instagram account, but 70% of these accounts don’t have a publishing method for posting on Instagram. It means that out of all the Instagram accounts connected to Levuro, 70% can’t publish posts using Levuro. As a result, Instagram users are deleting their accounts.

Impact on the business

More than half of Levuro users have an Instagram account. We are now losing a lot of them. The competition for social media tools is very high. If they can’t publish to Instagram easily, they will switch to another tool. By improving the user experience, we will reduce churn and grow the number of users that might convert to a paid account.

Research: Technical limitations

Publishing to Instagram through third-party tools and API limitations

There is 3 types of Instagram profiles:

  • Business account
  • Private or Creator account

Depending on your profile type, you can publish your social media post automatically or manually with push notifications on your phone:

  • Automatically with auto-publishing, it works only for Instagram business profiles. And the Instagram profile needs to be connected to a Facebook page.
  • Manually, using their phone, we call it mobile notification publishing. When it’s time to publish, they receive a notification on their phone to publish, it works for all Instagram profiles: private/creator and business.

Research: Gathering insights

Gathering data from beta users, usability testing, analytics, and customer support revealed multiple pain points and high frustration from users.

I used different methods to gather insights:

  • User feedback from customer support
  • Usability testing and user interview
  • Analyze user session recording

We uncovered more than 10 bugs that were part of the usability problems, but the main pain point was in the user flow. Users struggle to understand what they must do to define a publishing method.

Research insights

Finding 1: Users don’t understand why they must define a publishing method for their Instagram profile. The few users trying to accomplish it struggle to follow and understand the instructions.

  • Users don’t know if their Instagram profile is connected to Levuro.
  • Users don’t understand why they must choose a publishing method immediately without initiating the action. Their main goal was still to connect their IG profile.
  • There is a lack of clarity in the modal dialogue. Most users could not understand what it was about or what they had to do, e.g. it needed to be more evident to users that for auto publishing to work, their Instagram profile needs to be connected to a Facebook page. It led to users not understanding why it was not working and not knowing why.

On the user flow below, you can see the user's pain points when they try to define a publishing method for their Instagram profiles.

Finding 2: Users can’t create a post if they cannot define a publishing method. They are stuck, and they can’t use Levuro.

  • If users cannot specify a publishing method, they can’t open the composer to create a post.
  • Users never had the chance to do what they came to Levuro for: make and schedule a post for social media.
  • Some users could not understand the indications. Instead, they disconnected their Instagram profile, reconnected, and needed help understanding why they could not enter the composer.

Finding 3: Some users that published with mobile notifications never received a notification on their phone. They could not send another notification easily.

  • They tried to publish by opening the Levuro mobile app directly but it did not work.

There is no easy way to send another push notification if users didn’t receive the first one or made a mistake while opening it.

In conclusion, defining a publishing method is difficult for users because they do not understand why they need to do it. The explanation texts are confusing and not clear. During onboarding, the difficulty and frustration due to this process lead to a high abandonment rate from Instagram users.

Product & Design Decisions: A new user flow

Levuro users don’t want to publish manually. When possible, they want to post automatically. But we don’t allow all users to leverage this feature.

Who can publish automatically on Levuro:

  • Business profile: YES
  • Private or Creator account: NO

Due to API limitations, only business profiles can publish automatically. To allow all users to leverage this feature, We need to guide them into converting their profile to Business and inform them about the value of automatic publishing.

Missing value in the current user flow: Personal and Creator profiles can’t publish automatically on Levuro

With the new purple user flow, Business, Personal and Creator profiles can publish automatically on Levuro


Results: A completely new user flow where users can choose how they want to publish their Instagram social media posts and where we guide them to convert their profile to business if they want their posts to be published automatically.

Design: UX Writing

Giving users a choice on how they want to publish their posts and showing them the way to automatic publishing

Underneath are the new modal dialogs allowing users to choose how they want to publish. With the text, we explained the difference between the 2 methods and indicated what they needed to do to use Auto-publishing.

Rewriting confusing modal dialog.

Most users could not understand what was written, as a result the setting up of a publishing method often failed. Our goal rewriting modals were:

  • Guide users each step of the way to connect their profiles and define a publishing method.
  • Remove technical jargon to increase user confidence in completing the process.
  • Use a language that we use for talking that is dynamic and easy to understand.
  • Focus on writing clear and concise statements in case of errors or problems and provide a solution so the user can handle the situation.
You can see below the modal before and after.

Design: Usability improvements

Removing barriers: Allowing users to create their posts for social media even without a publishing method.

Letting users create the perfect social media post in Levuro and letting them define a publishing method when they wanted to schedule the post was more natural in the user flow. Plus, it gave the users motivation. Defining a publishing method would allow the user to publish the post he invested time in creating.


Users still have a reminder and the possibility to define a publishing method before they create their posts.


We removed the content on the right that was a repetition of the middle part. Instead, we invited the user to read our thorough help article about publishing to Instagram. And we mentioned that the support chat was available to help.

Allowing users to send the mobile notification a second time from the web app.

An easy way to publish from the mobile app if users don’t receive the push notification.

Conclusion

After 1 month of implementation:

  • Help requests from users to schedule a publishing method for Instagram are significantly reduced.
  • Screen recordings shows that most users could resolve the issues (connect the Instagram profile to a Facebook page, convert to Instagram business) by following indication of the different modal dialogs
  • We can already see a rise in Instagram accounts with a publishing method defined.

What’s next?

We’ll keep monitoring the success rate to define a publishing method for Instagram users. Since the Instagram API is constantly changing, we need to ensure the process is still relevant and clear, e.g., since a few weeks, reels can be published automatically through third-party tools like Levuro. It means that we need to identify whether it requires any changes in the user flow and how to integrate this new functionality in this process, how to make users aware of it, and possible modification or creation of help articles.
